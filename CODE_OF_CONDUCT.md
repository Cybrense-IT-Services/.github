# Community Code of Conduct

Cybrense open-source spaces are for constructive, professional collaboration.
Everyone who participates is expected to help keep them respectful and safe.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and considerate.
- Discuss ideas and evidence without attacking people.
- Give and receive technical feedback in good faith.
- Welcome contributors with different backgrounds and levels of experience.
- Protect private information and follow responsible security disclosure.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about project scope and moderation.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or hateful language.
- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Deliberately unsafe technical behavior, including exposing credentials or
  vulnerabilities publicly.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, issues, or other
content that violates this policy. They may also issue a warning or temporarily
or permanently restrict participation, depending on the severity and pattern
of behavior.

To report conduct privately, email
[contact@cybrense.com](mailto:contact@cybrense.com). Include relevant links or
context. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially as practical.

This code applies in all Cybrense project spaces and when someone is officially
representing a Cybrense community.
